## The TRACER level scale

The TRACER Scale comprises 9 levels, detailed below:

| Level | Name | General description |
|-------|------|---------------------|
| **TRACER Level 1** | Basic research / concept & market definition | Concept clearly defined; prior art searched; potential users, beneficiaries, and industry relevance identified. |
| **TRACER Level 2** | Laboratory formulation / design & prototype development | Initial design, formulation, or system architecture developed. Preliminary user/market requirements assessed. |
| **TRACER Level 3** | Optimization / functional testing / feeding trial | Working prototype created. Initial functional, analytical, or technical testing conducted. Industry relevance reassessed. IP protection initiated. |
| **TRACER Level 4** | Controlled validation & IP filing | Prototype validated under controlled conditions. Performance confirmed. IP filed. Market viability assessed via secondary data. Potential industry partner identified. |
| **TRACER Level 5** | Pilot preparation / pilot operation / beta testing | Pilot-scale testing or semi-field trials conducted. User manuals or operation guides prepared. Initial industry meetings held. BMC completed. Promotion/demonstration conducted. |
| **TRACER Level 6** | Pilot production / field testing / initial industry deployment | Agreements with industry partners secured. Performance validated under multiple conditions. Scalable manufacturing or propagation protocols prepared. Regulatory preparation initiated. |
| **TRACER Level 7** | Industry validation & regulatory preparation | Successful industry-scale validation achieved. Standardized production/fabrication protocols established. Licensing intent secured. FTO, IP valuation, and commercialization plan prepared. Regulatory approvals initiated. |
| **TRACER Level 8** | Commercial production readiness | Full-scale commercial production validated and replicable. Regulatory approvals obtained. Licensing terms negotiated. Fairness opinion review requested. |
| **TRACER Level 9** | Full commercialization | Technology licensed. Product, system, material, plant, or animal breed fully commercialized and available to target users. |

### How TRACER levels appear in results

After completing the questionnaire, the system assigns a TRACER level (1–9) based on the responses provided. The assigned level reflects the current readiness and commercialization stage of the assessed technology.

Each result page displays:

- **Assigned TRACER level** — the level number and name
- **Level description** — what the assigned level means in context
- **Insights** — observations drawn from the questionnaire responses
- **AI recommendation** — suggested next steps to progress toward the next TRACER level

### Visual scale

The results page renders the TRACER scale as a progress indicator, highlighting the assigned level and greying out levels not yet reached.
